ABOUT ME

My name is Hannika, and I am an enthusiastic full-stack web development student based in Helsinki, Finland, with a special interest in frontend development and UI/UX design. As a creative individual with strong visual skills, I find joy in crafting visually appealing and user-friendly interfaces.

In addition to my pursuit of web development, I am also a photographer under my own brand, Hannika Gabrielsson. I am known for my meticulous attention to detail, always striving for flawless execution and smooth functionality in my work.

Coding intrigues me because it challenges my logical and strategic thinking. Beyond programming, I find enjoyment in spending time in nature, dancing salsa, indulging in novels, and watching movies. Sustainable development and nature conservation are causes close to my heart.

With fluency in five languages and a background in linguistics — having graduated as a Spanish translator from the University of Turku in 2010 — I bring a diverse skill set to my studies at Business College Helsinki, where I am currently immersed in web development coursework. I eagerly embrace any challenges that this path presents.

PROJECTS & EXERCISES

PROJECT - Rakennusten kaupunki

In this group project in Business College we created an application where a user can find different buildings on a map and read information on different buildings. The user can also create an account to mark buildings as favourites.

I was responsible for designing the UI/UX design. I wanted to create a layout that best serves users' needs and gives the best user experience. I was responsible for the CSS and also made some additions to the project with React.

PROJECT - HappyNest

In this group project in Business College we created a community website for residents of an imaginary housing association. I was responsible for creating the brand that gave the guidelines for the content and sturcture. I was also responsible for designing the website structure and layout. I lead the project which was an interesting challenge and taught a lot about team work.

In this site we used HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript. The site is made mainly for desktop. Testing the site revealed a few errors and the next step in this project would be fixing them.
